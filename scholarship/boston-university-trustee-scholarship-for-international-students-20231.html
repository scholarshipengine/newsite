Boston University Trustee Scholarship

Boston University Trustee Scholarship

About Be Bold No-Essay Scholarship program

Each year, Boston University (BU) warmly embraces around 20 exceptional students who are invited to participate in the highly esteemed Trustee Scholars Program. A significant number of our present Scholars have demonstrated remarkable academic prowess in high school, maintaining flawless 4.0 grade point averages and securing top positions within their graduating class.

To qualify for a Trustee Scholarship, interested individuals must fulfill the following requirements: First, submit the Common Application along with all necessary documents for admission to any of the undergraduate degree programs offered at Boston University (BU) before the deadline of December 1. Second, ensure that the Trustee Scholarship essay, which is a vital component of the BU application, is fully completed on the Common Application. By meeting these two criteria, applicants will be considered for the prestigious Trustee Scholarship.

As part of your application through the Common Application, the Trustee Scholarship Essay holds significant importance. You are required to select one of the two prompts provided and submit an essay of 600 words or less. The first prompt reflects on the words of Nobel laureate and BU professor Elie Wiesel, who emphasized the beauty of learning and the acknowledgment that life did not begin at our birth. Wiesel highlights the impact of previous generations on our lives, drawing attention to books, films, podcasts, or life experiences that have deepened our connection to our personal history and identity. In your response, you should discuss the specific work or experience that has enhanced this connection and share the most valuable lesson you learned from it.

The second prompt asks you to recount a time when you found yourself outside your comfort zone or marginalized in a particular situation. You should vividly describe this experience and reflect on how you responded to the moment. Additionally, explain how this incident has influenced your subsequent actions and decisions. This prompt allows you to demonstrate resilience, adaptability, and personal growth in the face of adversity or discomfort. Consider sharing insights into your character development and the lessons you learned from navigating this challenging situation. Both prompts provide opportunities to showcase your unique perspectives, experiences, and personal growth in your application for the Trustee Scholarship.
