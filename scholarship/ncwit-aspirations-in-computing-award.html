NCWIT Aspirations in Computing Award program, Eligibility, Deadline - Closes 6.Nov.2023

About The NCWIT Aspirations in Computing Award program.

The NCWIT Aspirations in Computing (AiC) Award celebrates the accomplishments and interests of 9th-12th grade women, genderqueer, or non-binary students in the field of computing, while also inspiring them to pursue their passions. This prestigious award recognizes individuals based on their aptitude and aspirations in technology and computing, as demonstrated by their experience, activities, leadership, resilience in overcoming barriers, and future plans. Since its inception in 2007, over 25,000 students have been honored with an AiC Award. This award program follows a comprehensive structure that includes Winner, Honorable Mention, and Rising Star distinctions at both the National and Regional Affiliate levels. It encompasses all 50 states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, Guam, U.S. overseas military bases, and Canada.

The Regional Affiliate Award programs are organized in 79 locations across the country by member organizations of the NCWIT Alliance, a nationwide network comprising universities, companies, non-profits, and government organizations committed to promoting the influence and meaningful involvement of girls and women from diverse communities. The generous sponsorship of the NCWIT Award for AiC comes from esteemed partners such as Apple, Bank of America, Microsoft, and the Motorola Solutions Foundation. Their support contributes significantly to the recognition and advancement of young talents in the computing field.

Eligibility:

  • Users must be at least 13 years old when they create their account.
  • Applicants must obtain parental or guardian approval by the designated approval deadline, or they must be 18 years of age or older when submitting their application.
  • Applicants must be enrolled in a school located in the United States, Puerto Rico, Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, or on a U.S. overseas military base.
  • Have a U.S. Tax Identification or Social Security Number.
  • U.S Citizen

What to look out for:

  • Applicants have the opportunity to reapply each year. However, it's important to note that National Award Winners are ineligible to receive the award again in subsequent years.
  • Regional Affiliate Award programs may permit repeat recipients at their discretion.
