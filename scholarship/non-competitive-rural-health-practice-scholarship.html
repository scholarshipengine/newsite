Non-Competitive Rural Health Practice Scholarship Application, Eligibility - Closes 31.July.2023

About the Non-Competitive Rural Health Practice Scholarship programme.

The aim of this non-competitive scholarship is to attract and retain high-quality students for the Julia Jones Matthews School of Population and Public Health's online MPH program throughout Texas. The scholarship program also seeks to enhance the public health workforce in rural communities or rural organizations. The scholarship amount is $4,000, disbursed in two installments. In the first semester, students receive $1,500, and upon conducting their Applied Practice Experience (APE) at a rural facility, an additional $2,500 is granted. The number of scholarships awarded in each round is determined by the Department of Public Health program within the JJMSPPH, taking into account the availability of funds.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • During the first semester, students are required to complete a course project in either GSPH 5316 (for fall enrollees) or GSPH 5310 (for spring enrollees). This project should involve collaborating with a rural-serving organization or addressing a rural issue, as determined suitable by the instructor. Summer enrollees will have the opportunity to take one of the aforementioned classes based on availability.
  • As a requirement, students must commit to conducting their Applied Practice Experience (APE) in a public health or healthcare facility situated in a geographic region classified as rural. These designated rural sites are identified by utilizing the Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) rural analyzer: https://data.hrsa.gov/tools/rural-health. The MPH program will provide support in finding an appropriate site for the APE. Eligibility is extended to students residing in urban areas as well, although they are advised to utilize the aforementioned website to determine their proximity to such rural sites.
  • The facilities or clinics where students are hosted will be requested to submit a brief statement to the Executive Vice President and Director of the F. Marie Hall Institute for Rural and Community Health, highlighting the impact made by the student during their time at the facility
  • To retain the scholarship, students must maintain satisfactory progress on their MPH degree plan and uphold a good academic record, which entails maintaining a GPA of 3.0 or higher. If a student falls into academic probation, the scholarship will be forfeited for the subsequent semester. However, there is an opportunity for scholarship renewal once the student achieves a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher.
