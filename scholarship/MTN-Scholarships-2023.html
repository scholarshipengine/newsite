MTN Scholarships 2023 - The MTN Science and Technology Scholarship (STS) & MTN Scholarship for Blind Students (SBS)

The MTN Science and Technology Scholarship (STS) is a program specifically designed to support students in the field of science and technology. The application is open to two groups of students: those who have completed their second semester 200 level examinations and will be advancing to the 300 level, and students who are currently in their 300 level of study. This flexibility acknowledges the variation in academic calendars among public tertiary institutions. By considering students at different stages of their academic journey, the MTN STS aims to provide opportunities for a wider range of students pursuing science and technology disciplines, ensuring that deserving individuals receive support and recognition for their dedication and achievements.

The MTN Scholarship for Blind Students (SBS) is a dedicated program aimed at supporting blind students pursuing their education in Nigerian Public Tertiary Institutions, including universities, polytechnics, and colleges of education. The application is open to blind students at both the 200 and 300 level of their studies. However, for blind students specifically studying Law, which is a five-year course, only those at the 300 level are eligible to apply for the MTN Scholarship. This program recognizes the unique challenges faced by blind students and aims to provide them with financial assistance and support to further their education. By offering opportunities at different academic levels, the MTN SBS ensures that blind students across various stages of their academic journey have the chance to receive this valuable scholarship and pursue their educational goals.

The MTN Science and Technology Scholarship (STS): Eligibility requirements for the MTN Scholarships program vary based on the academic level and institution type. For STEM students in public universities and colleges of education, a minimum Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) score of 3.0/4 or 3.5/5 (second class upper credit) is necessary. STEM students in public polytechnics must have a minimum CGPA of 3.0 (upper credit) from the Ordinary National Diploma (OND) Program and must have obtained admission into the Higher National Diploma (HND) Program. Direct entry level STEM students are required to have a minimum CGPA of 3.0 (upper credit) from the Ordinary National Diploma (OND) program and must have secured admission into the 300 level/3rd year in a public university. These criteria ensure that deserving students who have demonstrated excellence in their academic pursuits are considered for the MTN Scholarships.

MTN Scholarship for Blind Students (SBS): Blind students who wish to be eligible for the MTN Scholarships program have specific requirements based on their educational institutions. For blind students enrolled in universities or colleges of education, a minimum Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of 2.5 is required. Similarly, blind students attending public polytechnics must have a minimum CGPA of 2.0 from the Ordinary National Diploma (OND) Program and should have obtained admission into the Higher National Diploma (HND) Program. These criteria ensure that blind students who have shown dedication and academic performance are considered for the MTN Scholarships, providing them with opportunities to further their education and achieve their goals.
